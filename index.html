Phase 02 · The Primary Application

Investigating Algorithmic Biases

Establishing a credible, robust, and evidence-based methodology to analyse and respond to social media content patterns.

Read Full Context

Why This Matters: Algorithmic bias on social media platforms — particularly the disproportionate reach of right-wing and anti-Muslim content — is a live and pressing issue that Equi has been invited to address. Doing so with credibility requires a clear, structured, repeatable and well-governed methodology that helps establish Equi's preferred position in key discussions.

Approach & Method

Investigating bias within social media algorithms starts with the simple-sounding but difficult task of defining specific questions that require structured answers to give the evidence base needed.

This requires precision, governance and a clear understanding of what is possible - which we will manage using a method called the 'Value Pyramid'. Completing this step provides the structure for the clear, defensible approach that will be adopted to investigate algorithmic bias. The evidence base that follows needs to withstand the highest levels of scrutiny.

Understanding the Sources of Bias: Three Distinct Factors.

  • 1. Inauthentic Coordinated Behaviour Organised networks deliberately amplifying content — bots, coordinated inauthentic accounts, manipulation campaigns.
  • 2. Bias Patterns Platform architecture that structurally favours certain content types, emotional registers, or ideological framings.
  • 3. Content Engagement Patterns Legitimate user behaviour where certain content generates more interaction and is therefore surfaced more widely.
Phase 03 · The Apex of Engagement

Integrating AI Capabilities

Transferring critical technical skills and embedding AI workflows directly into Equi's core operating model.

Read Full Context

Why This Matters: Equi produces research that is careful, considered and impactful. The people who write it care deeply about the credibility of their work which serves as the foundation of Equi, its reputation and the experience of collaborating on key topics.

The cultural moment we're in that Equi's research could shape is evolving quickly. AI best practices (also evolving quickly) could modify how fast and how widely Equi can contribute - potentially making it possible to produce research in a fraction of the time currently required.

Skills transfer is embedded throughout the project, ensuring the capability stays with Equi and continues to develop after the engagement concludes. We will not produce the final report of the assignment by prompting an AI tool, but we will transparently compare and contrast the differences in real time with you in the meeting that concludes the work.

Approach

We will help Equi understand the external landscape for AI best practices for knowledge workers and corresponding internal mechanisms required. Specifically, what tools exist, how to evaluate and govern them, where the risks lie and how to manage them. We will map the research workflow end to end, identify entry points for AI, and help your team adopt AI with ownership and control - all while evidencing the tangible benefits by performing research using these tools for the algorithmic bias workstream.
